Director

Dr. Matthew S.W. Silk
Director, Ethics of AI Society of Waterloo

Dr. Silk is the founding Director of the Ethics of AI Society of Waterloo. He is the co-author of An Inquirer's Guide to Ethics in AI from Broadview Press. His work focuses on the philosophical, ethical, and political implications of artificial intelligence, with particular emphasis on responsibility, governance, and public accountability in emerging technologies.

He established EAISW to foster interdisciplinary dialogue between philosophy, computer science, public policy, and civil society.

Silk is also the host of the Reconstructing Philosophy Podcast and has written over one hundred ethical analyses for the Prindle Post . He also works more broadly in the philosophy of technology, including the ethics of outer space exploration .

Research & Intellectual Focus

  • AI ethics and democratic governance
  • Algorithmic responsibility and moral agency
  • AI literacy frameworks in higher education
  • Epistemology of artificial systems
  • Technology, trust, and institutional legitimacy

Role within EAISW

As Director, Dr. Silk oversees the society’s programming, partnerships, and long-term intellectual vision. This includes organizing symposiums, coordinating interdisciplinary collaborations, and developing public-facing resources on AI literacy and ethical governance.

The Director also works to connect academic research with policy discussions and public education initiatives.
